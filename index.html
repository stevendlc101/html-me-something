Casablanca

Casablanca is an American film made in 1942 by director Michael Curtiz. The film was produced by Warner Brothers, and it was based on the play Everybody Comes to Rick's. The film is regarded by many critics as one of the best classic American films. There are many aspects that make Casablanca a fantastic film.

The Cast

  • Humphrey Bogart
  • Claude Rains
  • Peter Lorre

  • Sydney Greenstreet

  • Dooley Wilson

The Lines

The script of Casablanca is extermely well written. In additon, it contains many memorable lines of dialog, including:

  • "Of all the gin joints, in all the towns, in all the world, she walks into mine.""
  • "We'll always have Paris."
  • "Maybe not today. Maybe not tomorrow, but soon. And for the rest of your life."
  • "It doesn't take much to see that the problems of three little people don't amount to a hill of beans in this crazy world."
  • "Here's looking at you kid."
  • "Round up the usual suspects."
  • ...and perhaps the most mis-quoted movie line in history: "Play it, Sam. Play As Time Goes By"

    • The Song

    As Time Goes By has grown to be as iconic a song as Casablanca has grown to be an iconic film. It captures the pain and sweetness of an unrequited love. While Dooley Wilson (who played Sam) received the honor of playing the song in the film, many artist have covered the song throught the past 70 plus years.
